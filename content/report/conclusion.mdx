## 4. Conclusion

### 4.1 Figures of Merit — Brayton & Heat Rejection

- **Thermal efficiency:** ~35% (10.1 MW from 30 MW).
- **Parasitic load (air-cooling):** ~50 kW (0.3% of heat rejected, 0.5% of turbine output).

### 4.2 Figures of Merit — Carbon Reduction

- **CO₂ displaced (nuclear vs coal):** ~70,000 tonnes/year.
- **CO₂ captured (DAC):** 1100 tonnes/year.
- **Parasitic regeneration load:** 0.035 MWe (0.35%).
- **Thermal regeneration (waste heat):** 0.18 MWth.
- **Pressure-drop parasitic:** 0.42 MWe (4.2%).
- **Total parasitic:** 0.45 MWe (4.5%).

Employing waste heat for regeneration decreased the DAC parasitic load by ~28%. The cost of parasitic load can be partially offset by carbon credits (~£90/t-CO₂).
